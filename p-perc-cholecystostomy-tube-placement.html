First, Test Yourself!

Indications

Decompression with cholecystostomy tube placement is often palliative.

MOST COMMON INDICATION

Consider in

  • critically ill patients when surgical intervention is not possible (in conjunction with antibiotics)
  • patients managed nonoperatively who experience disease progression or no appreciable improvement after 1-3 days of management with antibiotics

Temporizing measure — whenever possible, get patient to surgery

Treatment with percutaneous cholecystostomy is typically definitive

Contraindications

Absolute

None! (Patients are typically critically ill)

Relative

  • Coagulopathy
  • Severe bleeding diathesis
  • Gallbladder tumor with risk of seeding
  • Drain placement may be challenging in the case of
    • Perforated gallbladder
    • Porcelain gallbladder
    • Gallbladder filled with stones
    • Interposed bowel or colon precluding safe access

Percutaneous Cholecystostomy/Question Set 01 - Indications
A 68-year-old patient presents with right upper quadrant pain and a distended gallbladder with wall thickening seen on US. The patient is not a surgical cantidate. There is also moderate ascites.
Given these findings, is it okay to proceed with percutaneous drainage?

YES — ascites is not a contraindication to percutaneous gallbladder drainage.
Incidence of failure of tract maturation (major concern) in patients with ascites is similar to rates in those without ascites
